How It Works

Someone lands on the page, answers 6 questions about their financial operations, and gets a personalized maturity score. To see their full results, they give you their contact info. You get everything automatically.

The Journey Your Visitor Takes

  1. Landing page — A headline that grabs the right person and filters out the wrong ones
  2. 6-question quiz — Questions designed to reveal their pain points while keeping them engaged
  3. Contact capture — They enter name, email, and company to unlock their results
  4. Personalized results — A maturity score with insights tailored to their industry and answers

Takes about 90 seconds. By the time they finish, you know their pain points, their situation, and how urgently they need help — before you ever get on a call.

The Scoring System

Every person who completes the quiz gets a Financial Operations Maturity Score between 5 and 95. The score isn't random — it's calculated based on their actual answers:

  • How current their books are — up to date vs. months behind vs. never closed
  • Business complexity — one entity vs. multiple companies to track
  • Where they feel the pain — visibility issues, trust problems, founder time drain
  • What it's costing them — missed financing, lost deals, tax surprises
  • Time investment — fully delegated vs. consuming their nights and weekends

This gives you instant lead qualification. Someone scoring 25 with urgent pain signals is a different conversation than someone scoring 72 who's "just exploring."

Industry-Specific Results

The results page adapts based on which industry they selected:

  • Entertainment / Media / Production
  • Professional Services
  • E-commerce / DTC
  • Multi-business Operator

Each path shows case studies and dollar-impact estimates that actually resonate with their world. A film producer sees different examples than an e-commerce founder.

The "Cost of Waiting" Calculator

On the results page, there's an interactive calculator that shows prospects how much money they're likely losing by not fixing their financial operations. It factors in:

  • Their annual revenue
  • How far behind their books are
  • Whether they've been denied financing

Nothing creates urgency like a number. "You're leaving an estimated $47,000 on the table annually" hits differently than "you should probably fix this."

Where Your Leads Go

Every completed quiz automatically captures:

  • Name, email, and company
  • Every answer they gave
  • Their calculated maturity score
  • Lead tier (A/B/C based on score and urgency)
  • Pain level and urgency indicators
  • Which campaign brought them (if you use tracking links)
  • Timestamp

All of this lands in your Google Sheet automatically. You also get an email notification at dean@routeoneadvisory.com every time someone completes the quiz.

Ideas for Future Versions

The quiz works great as-is, but here are questions we could add to make lead qualification even sharper:

"What's your approximate annual revenue?"

Options: Under $1M, $1-5M, $5-20M, $20-50M, $50M+

This lets you prioritize bigger opportunities and tailor your pricing conversations.

"How are your financials currently handled?"

Options: In-house bookkeeper, External accountant, Outsourced firm, DIY/Founder, Hybrid

Helps you position Route One against whatever they're currently doing.

"When are you looking to make a change?"

Options: Immediately, This quarter, This year, Just exploring

Hot leads get immediate follow-up. Explorers go into a nurture sequence.

"What stage is your company at?"

Options: Solo founder, Small team (2-10), Growth stage (11-50), Scaled (50+)

Adjusts how you talk about complexity and what solutions make sense.

Each additional question adds about 15 seconds. Worth testing one or two at a time to make sure completion rates stay high.

The Bigger Idea: Intent-Based Funnels

This quiz isn't just a lead capture tool. It's a proof of concept for something bigger.

Here's the backstory: Justin referred this project, and your friend Matt — an amazing full-stack developer — first showed me two tools that changed everything for me: V0 (an AI code generator) and Firecrawl (a web scraping tool). A few weeks later, Matt shared his personal site. Hidden in it was an Easter egg — a small interactive element that made me stop and engage differently than I would with a normal page.

That's when something clicked.

The Easter egg didn't just show me information. It made me reveal my own intent through interaction. I wasn't passively reading — I was actively participating, and in doing so, I was telling the page exactly what I cared about.

The Hypothesis

You already know intent data. You use it every day to target ads — keywords, behaviors, lookalikes, signals that suggest someone is in-market.

But here's the gap: intent data gets you the click. What happens after the click is usually static.

The landing page is the same for everyone. The messaging is generic. You're guessing at what they care about based on what ad they clicked — but you don't actually know until they convert (if they convert).

Traditional Funnel

  • Intent data targets the ad
  • Static landing page
  • Same message for everyone
  • Hope they convert
  • Qualify after conversion

Intent-Based Funnel

  • Intent data targets the ad
  • Interactive quiz captures intent
  • Results adapt to their answers
  • They see themselves in the output
  • Pre-qualified before conversion

When you match intent-targeted media buying with an intent-based funnel, something powerful happens:

  • The ad speaks to their problem (intent data)
  • The funnel makes them articulate their problem (intent capture)
  • The results prove you understand their specific situation (intent reflection)

Every step reinforces the last. The alignment compounds.

This quiz is the first test of that hypothesis. It captures intent in real-time, adapts the output to what they told you, and delivers them pre-qualified to your pipeline.

If this works — and the data suggests it can — this becomes a template. Different industries, different pain points, different entry points. All feeding the same system. All running the same playbook.

What This Opens Up

This funnel isn't the end. It's the beginning.

  • Vertical-specific funnels — One for entertainment, one for e-commerce, one for professional services. Same core engine, different messaging.
  • Campaign-matched creative — Ad copy that mirrors quiz language. Seamless intent thread from impression to conversion.
  • Automated follow-up based on answers — Not just "thanks for taking the quiz" — emails that reference exactly what they said.
  • Retargeting with context — Know someone scored 28 with urgent pain? That's a different retargeting message than someone who scored 65 and is "just exploring."

The system learns. The system scales. The system remembers what each person told you.
